Frequently Asked Questions

To be eligible to donate blood, you must be at least 17 years old, weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good health.

You can donate whole blood every 56 days. Platelets can be donated more frequently, usually every 7 days.

Blood donation can help save lives, and it also has health benefits such as reducing the risk of heart disease and improving cardiovascular health.

The process involves hospitals notifying blood banks of the need for blood, which is then matched with donors.

Certain conditions may disqualify you from donating, such as recent surgeries, infections, or certain medications.

The donation process is safe, and side effects are usually minor such as slight dizziness or bruising.

It’s important to know your blood type as it determines compatibility with recipients during transfusions.

You can register to donate by visiting our website or contacting your local blood bank.

The coordinator dashboard provides insights and information on donation drives and donor data.
